About Safe Journey

Safe Journey is dedicated to providing evidence-based information about psychedelics with a focus on harm reduction and safety. Our goal is to help reduce risks associated with psychedelic use through education and awareness.

While psychedelics can offer profound experiences and potential therapeutic benefits when used responsibly, they also carry risks that should be understood and respected.

Safety Guidelines

Set & Setting

"Set" refers to your mindset, intentions, and emotional state. "Setting" is your physical environment and social context.

Choose a comfortable, safe environment with trusted people. Avoid using psychedelics when in a negative emotional state or dealing with serious mental health issues.

Dosage

Start with low doses, especially if you're inexperienced. Different substances have different potency levels.

Use a reliable scale for measurement when applicable. Remember that potency can vary significantly between batches.

Trip Sitter

Having a sober, experienced, and trusted person present can significantly increase safety.

A good trip sitter knows when to help and when to give space, and understands how to handle difficult experiences.

Drug Interactions

Psychedelics can interact dangerously with many medications and other substances.

Research potential interactions before combining substances. Particularly dangerous combinations include MAOIs, lithium, and stimulants.

Medical Considerations

Certain medical and psychological conditions may increase risks.

Those with personal or family history of psychosis, schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, or heart conditions should exercise extreme caution or abstain.

Testing

Always test substances using reagent test kits to verify their identity.

This is especially important with substances obtained from unregulated sources.

Common Psychedelic Substances

Psilocybin Mushrooms

Duration: 4-6 hours

Onset: 20-60 minutes

Safety considerations: Generally physiologically safe but can cause profound psychological experiences. Start with low doses (0.5-1g dried) for beginners.

LSD

Duration: 8-12 hours

Onset: 30-90 minutes

Safety considerations: Long duration requires proper time commitment. Test tabs as many substances are misrepresented as LSD. Start with low doses (50-75Î¼g) for beginners.

DMT

Duration: 15-30 minutes (smoked)

Onset: Almost immediate

Safety considerations: Very intense but short-lasting experience. Having a sitter is essential. Proper vaporization technique is important.

MDMA

Duration: 3-5 hours

Onset: 30-60 minutes

Safety considerations: Test for purity. Stay hydrated but don't overhydrate. Avoid frequent use due to neurotoxicity concerns. Allow 2-3 months between experiences.

Preparation

Before Your Experience

Proper preparation can significantly improve safety and outcomes:

  • Research the substance thoroughly
  • Set clear intentions for the experience
  • Prepare your environment (clean space, comfortable seating, access to water)
  • Create a playlist of appropriate music
  • Have light, nutritious food available for after the experience
  • Clear your schedule for the experience and the following day
  • Consider having a journal ready for insights
  • Inform your trip sitter about any personal concerns

Integration

After Your Experience

Integration is the process of making meaning from and incorporating insights from psychedelic experiences into daily life:

  • Journal about your experience
  • Take time to reflect before making major life decisions
  • Consider speaking with an integration specialist or therapist
  • Join integration circles or community groups
  • Implement small, sustainable changes based on insights
  • Practice meditation or other mindfulness techniques
  • Create art or other creative expressions of your experience
